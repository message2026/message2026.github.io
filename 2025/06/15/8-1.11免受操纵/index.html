第十一章：免受操纵

发表于2025-06-15|更新于2025-08-22|来自上帝的新讯息其他教导
|总字数:14.3 k|阅读时长:59 分钟

Freedom From Manipulation

As received by Marshall Vian Summers on October 7, 2008 in Boulder, Colorado
2008 年 10 月 7 日，在科罗拉多州博尔德(Boulder, Colorado)，向上帝的信使马歇尔・维安・萨默斯(Marshall Vian Summers)揭示

You live in a mental environment as well as a physical environment. The physical environment is the world that your senses report—a world that you can see, hear, touch and feel; a world that you must navigate using your intellect and your physical skills. But the mental environment is more subtle. It is the environment of thought and influence.

你生活在一个心理环境和一个物理环境中。物理环境是你的感官所报告的世界——一个你能够看到、听到、触摸到和感受到的世界；一个你必须运用你的智力和你的身体技能去探索的世界。但心理环境更加微妙。它是思想和影响力的环境。

Go into any home or place of business and you will experience the unique mental environment of that place. This is part of your daily experience. People describe this in many ways, of course. But fundamentally they are feeling something very different being in different environments.

走进任何家庭或商业场所，你都会感受到那里独特的心理氛围。这是你日常生活的一部分。当然，人们对此有很多描述。但从根本上说，他们在不同的环境中感受到的是截然不同的感受。

If you were able to go into every house on any street, you would find a unique mental environment within their home, depending upon the nature of the relationships that exist there and the mental health of those who live there. Whenever you have a private conversation with someone, you create your own mental environment with that person. Here even in a restaurant, you can go from table to table, and there is a slight mental environment created at each one.

如果你能走进任何一条街上的每一户人家，你会发现他们家里都有一种独特的心理环境，这取决于那里关系的性质以及居住者的心理健康状况。每当你与某人进行私人交谈时，你都会与对方共同创造你们自己的心理环境。即使在餐厅里，你从一张桌子走到另一张桌子，每张桌子都会创造出一种微妙的心理环境。

Ultimately, you as an individual create a mental environment around you, depending upon the nature of your thinking and emotions. Here people walk around with a cloud over them, enveloped in their own fear, anxiety or resistance. Or they can create much the opposite—there is a feeling of openness, trust and safety around them.

最终，你作为个体，会根据你的思维和情绪的自然，在自身周围创造一种心理环境。在这里，人们仿佛笼罩着一层阴云，被他们自身的恐惧、焦虑或抵触所笼罩。或者，他们也可以创造出截然相反的景象——周围弥漫着一种开放、信任和安全的感觉。

While you create your own mental environment to a slight degree, you are living in the mental environment that everyone is creating and continues to create every moment of every day. This is the world that humanity has created, not only as a physical landscape, but as a complex and immense mental environment as well.

当你在轻微程度上创造你自己的心理环境时，你正生活在每个人都在创造、并且每时每刻都在持续创造的心理环境中。这是人类创造的世界，它不仅是一个物质景观，更是一个复杂且庞大的心理环境。

It is within this larger environment that there are powerful influences—cultural influences, political influences, commercial influences, religious influences and the influences of powerful individuals in one’s life.

正是在这个大环境中，存在着强大的影响力——文化的影响、政治的影响、商业的影响、宗教的影响以及一个人生活中强大个体的影响。

Here there are no neutral mental environments. For that, you must seek refuge in nature. But even in nature there can be subtle differences in environments, depending upon the creatures that live there and other forces in the mental environment that are beyond your visible range. People, however, generally seek refuge in nature to experience a neutral mental environment, an environment that is not activated or strongly influenced by human thought, feelings and emphasis.

这里没有中性的心理环境。为此，你必须在自然中寻求庇护。但即使在自然中，环境也可能存在微妙的差异，这取决于生活在那里的生物，以及心理环境中超出你可见范围的其他力量。然而，人们通常寻求自然的庇护，是为了体验一种中性的心理环境，一种不受人类思想、感受和强调所激活或强烈影响的环境。

People know very little about the mental environment, of course, and so they often lack discernment here and discretion. They become involved with people or environments that are generally unhealthy for them because there are no neutral mental environments where people are concerned.

当然，人们对心理环境知之甚少，因此他们往往缺乏辨识力和判断力。他们与那些通常对他们不健康的人或环境纠缠不清，因为在人们关注的地方，不存在中立的心理环境。

Here who you associate with has a great bearing on your thoughts, your feelings and the degree to which you can become aware of yourself and your environment. Everyone is influencing everyone, but particularly those people with whom you are directly engaged. You can be in a mental environment, working there or living there or spending a great deal of time there, and the mental environment itself can have a profound influence upon you.

在这里，你与谁交往，对你的思想、你的感受，以及你对你自己和环境的觉知程度有着重大影响。每个人都在影响着每个人，尤其是那些与你直接互动的人。你可能身处一个心理环境，在那里工作、生活或在那里度过大量时间，而这个心理环境本身能够对你产生深远的影响。

If you spend much time with a group of people who have a particular point of view, which they reinforce for one another, you will find yourself over time being persuaded by their point of view, and may even adopt it yourself. If you spend time with a group of people who are highly critical of the government, for example, you will find yourself falling into their line of thinking. This is part of your social adaptation, but it is also the influence in the mental environment there.

如果你花大量时间与一群持有特定观点、并且彼此相互强化的人相处，你会发现你自己随着时间的推移会被他们的观点所说服，甚至可能你自己也采纳这些观点。例如，如果你与一群对政府持强烈批评态度的人相处，你会发现你自己会陷入他们的思维模式。这是你社会适应的一部分，但也是当地心理环境的影响。

You live in a sea of persuasion—everywhere around you, occurring on so many different levels. People are often greatly influenced by one other person in their life, or by their parents, someone whose influence and sway are inordinate and very strong, and they are given to submit to it. Here you may even find yourself feeling constantly overshadowed by someone else because in the dynamic of your relationship with them, you tend to yield and they tend to dominate. Sometimes this is unexpected and unanticipated. It is just the dynamic of the relationship.

你生活在一片说服的海洋之中——在你周围无处不在，发生在如此多不同的层面。人们常常深受他们生命中另一个人或父母的影响，这些人的影响力和支配力异常强大，而他们却不得不屈服于此。在这里，你甚至可能发现你自己不断地被他人的阴影所笼罩，因为在你与他们的关系动态中，你倾向于屈服，而他们倾向于支配。有时，这种情况会出乎你的意料之外。这只是关系的动态而已。

Certainly, a marriage and a family create a powerful mental environment, an environment of influence. In this regard, whoever you marry is really who you are choosing to be your primary influence in your life.

当然，婚姻和家庭会创造出一种强大的心理环境，一个充满影响力的环境。从这个意义上来说，你与谁婚姻，实际上就是你选择谁来成为你生命中最重要的影响力。

In people’s romantic pursuits, of course, this is almost never considered. But this is a chief factor in determining whether someone is an appropriate partner for you. You will be assigning them the role as your chief and primary influence if you unite with them in marriage or partnership. Do not think you are going to rehabilitate them through association with you. That is a very common mistake.

当然，在人们的浪漫追求中，这一点几乎从未被考虑过。但这却是决定某人是否适合你的主要因素。如果你与他们结为婚姻或伴侣，你就将他们视为你最主要的、最重要的影响者。不要认为通过与你的交往就能让他们改邪归正。这是一个非常常见的错误。

Who you surround yourself with represents your chief influences. Just as a political leader will surround themselves with a group of advisors who have great bearing on that individual’s decision making and perception, you as an individual surround yourself with people who will become your chief advisors and influences. Even if you do not wholly respect or agree with their views, they will still have great bearing on your own. This is the power of influence in the mental environment.

你身边的人代表着你的主要影响力。正如政治领袖会身边围绕着一群对其决策和认知有着巨大影响的顾问一样，你作为个体，身边也会围绕着一些会成为你主要顾问和影响者的人。即使你并不完全尊重或认同他们的观点，他们仍然会对你产生巨大的影响。这就是心理环境中影响力的力量。

Do not think here that you as an individual, by the sheer intensity of your will or by living defensively, that you can overcome all of these influences upon you. That is underestimating the power of the mental environment. And do not think that you can create your own reality to a very great extent here beyond trying to change the way you interpret your experiences because you are living in the reality that so many other people are creating along with you.

在这里，不要认为你作为一个个体，仅仅凭借你的意志力，或者通过防御性地生活，就能克服所有这些对你的影响。这是低估了心理环境的力量。不要认为你能够在很大程度上创造你自己的现实，在这里，除了试图改变你解读你体验现实的方式之外，没有现实，因为你正在与许多人一起生活在他们所创造的现实中。

This is one of the ways that people enter into fantasy or illusion. They create a set of ideas or expectations and reinforce these to such an extent that they are disassociated from the environment around them. But what they fail to see is that their disengagement does not mean that the general environment is not continuing to influence them significantly.

这是人们陷入幻想或幻觉的方式之一。他们会创造一系列想法或期望，并不断强化这些想法或期望，以至于他们与周围的环境脱节。但他们没有看见，他们的脱离并不意味着整体环境不再对他们产生重大影响。

If you spend time with people who are unsuccessful in any regard, you will begin to share their attitudes and their experiences. Do not think that you alone can uplift them or alter their thinking and behavior. The collective intentions of others is greater than your individual intention in this regard. As a result, your ability to achieve goals or success in life will be greatly dependent upon those individuals with whom you surround yourself and who have a chief influence or function in your life.

如果你花时间与那些在任何方面都不成功的人相处，你就会开始分享他们的态度和经验。不要认为你一个人就能提升他们，或改变他们的思维和行为。在这方面，其他人的集体意图远大于你个人的意图。因此，你能否实现在生命中的目标或取得成功，很大程度上取决于你身边那些对你的生命产生主要影响或作用的人。

This is the unseen power of the mental environment. Never underestimate it. It has a greater impact upon your thoughts and feelings than even the physical environment that you can see and discern with your senses.

这就是心理环境的无形力量。永远不要低估它。它对你的思想和情感的影响，甚至比你用感官所能看到和辨别的物理环境还要大。

Here you can begin to recognize the value of your own experience. For you have experienced many different kinds of mental environments, some of which were attractive and some of which were not and some of which were just confusing. You did not know how to interpret them.

在这里，你可以开始认识到你自身经验的价值。因为你体验过许多不同类型的心理环境，有些很吸引人，有些则不然，有些则令人困惑。你不知道该如何解读它们。

Being with certain people felt good. With others it did not. Sometimes it even was felt to be dangerous. This is part of your experience of living in the world. If you are more sensitive to these things, then your experience of the mental environment is more pronounced. This could be helpful to you if you learn to develop skill in the mental environment, which is something that very few people have achieved to any degree whatsoever.

和某些人在一起感觉很好。和其他人在一起感觉不好。有时甚至会觉得危险。这是你活在世上的经历的一部分。如果你对这些事情更敏感，你对心理环境的体验就会更强烈。如果你学会培养在心理环境中的技巧，这可能会对你有所帮助，而这恰恰是很少有人能够达到的。

Sometimes people that you meet are very attractive. They seem powerful or charming or very determined, and you are attracted to this aspect of them. Perhaps there are other qualities about them that are not so impressive at the outset that end up being critically important for you to discern. Should you pursue a relationship with this person for any reason, whether it be for personal companionship or for goals in your work, you will find that these unforeseen tendencies will have a great bearing on the outcome.

有时你遇到的人非常有魅力。他们看起来强大、迷人或意志坚定，而你被他们的这些特质所吸引。或许他们身上还有其他一些品质，一开始并不那么引人注目，但最终却对你至关重要，需要你去发现。无论你出于何种原因与这个人建立关系，无论是为了个人友谊还是为了工作目标，你都会发现，这些意想不到的特质会对结果产生重大影响。

If you observe people objectively without condemnation, without criticism, they will tell you a great deal about themselves. And if you learn about their past experience—what they have achieved, what they have not achieved, how they have dealt with uncertainty, how they have dealt with loss and disappointment, how they view their past relationships and so forth—you will learn a great deal about how they will function in the future and whether or not you should become engaged with them.

如果你客观地观察人们，不带谴责、不带批评，他们将会告诉你很多关于他们自己的事情。如果你了解他们过去的经历——他们取得了什么成就，他们没有取得什么成就，他们如何应对不确定性，他们如何应对损失和失望，他们如何看待他们过去的关系等等——你将了解到很多关于他们未来将如何运作，以及你是否应该与他们进行参与的信息。

Part of this is based upon their appearance, of course, and their behavior and the nature of their communication. But then there is the more subtle experience of someone. Someone can appear to be quite attractive in all the normal respects, but there is something about them that gives you caution, that makes you feel reserved, that is like a warning. If you do not look at someone with this greater sensitivity or heed the sense of restraint you might feel around them, you could make a critical mistake in participating with that person. And, of course, these mistakes are made continuously everywhere, in all realms of human engagement.

当然，这部分取决于他们的外貌、行为以及交流的自然。但此外，还有对某人更微妙的体验。某人可能在所有正常的方面都显得颇具吸引力，但他们身上却有某种东西让你感到谨慎，让你感到矜持，就像一种警告。如果你不以这种更敏锐的洞察力看待某人，或者不留意你在他们身边可能感受到的拘谨，你就可能在与此人交往时犯下致命的错误。当然，这些错误在人类交往的所有领域中无处不在、不断发生。

In innumerable ways people are trying to influence each other to achieve their goals and to get what they want. Sometimes this self-serving attitude is very overt. It is very obvious. People are manipulating others or trying to manipulate you to get you to buy what they want you to buy, or do what they want you to do, or to surrender to them or to agree with their point of view, or to join their political cause, or to adopt their own set of views.

人们试图以无数种方式相互影响，以实现目标，得到他们想要的东西。有时，这种自私自利的态度非常明显，显而易见。人们操纵他人或试图操纵你，让你买他们想让你买的东西，做他们想让你做的事情，屈服于他们，认同他们的观点，加入他们的政治事业，或者采纳他们自己的一套观点。

But even in more subtle ways, people want their relationships to support their goals. So it is very important in relationships that you become acquainted with the other person’s goals, not only the goals that they proclaim, but their more subtle ambitions as well.

但即使在更微妙的方式中，人们也希望他们的关系能够支持他们的目标。因此，在人际关系中，了解对方的目标至关重要，不仅是他们宣称的目标，还有他们更微妙的抱负。

Skill and discernment here are critically important. For failure in life largely occurs as a result of being with the wrong people, of not discerning the nature of the relationship fully, of not discerning the problems and the warning signs. It is fundamentally a problem of not following the deeper Knowledge that lives within you.

在这里，技巧和辨别力至关重要。因为生命的失败很大程度上源于与错误的人相处，源于未能充分辨识关系的自然，源于未能辨识问题和警示信号。从根本上来说，问题在于未能遵循你内在更深层次的内识。

The real secret here to being able to avoid manipulation and to avoid making critical mistakes in your relationships and in engagements with others is the discovery of Knowledge within yourself. Knowledge represents the deeper Mind within you, a Mind very unlike the mind that you think with; the mind that you use to speculate and compare, contrast and criticize. The deeper Mind within you is quiet. It is discerning. It is watchful. It is not conflicted, and it is not afraid of the world.

在这里，能够避免被操控，避免在你的人际关系和与他人的交往中犯下关键错误的真正秘诀，是发现你内在的内识。内识代表着你内在更深层次的心灵，一个与你用来思考的心灵截然不同的心灵；一个你用来推测、比较、对照和批评的心灵。你内在更深层次的心灵是安静的。它辨识。它警觉。它不冲突，它不惧怕世界。

It is so very different from your intellect, your social mind, your mind that has been conditioned by your environment, your family and your culture. There is really no comparison here. Knowledge is the Mind that God created in you. But your surface mind where you live every day is the mind the world has created in you. It represents the chief influences of your culture, and your responses and reactions to the world. It contains your desires, your fears, your regrets, your hostilities, your unforgiveness, your tendencies, areas where you are aware, areas where you are not aware and so forth.

它与你的智力、你的社会心灵、以及你被你的环境、你的家庭和你的文化所熏陶的心灵截然不同。这里真的没有可比性。内识是上帝在你内在创造的心灵。但你每天生活的表面心灵，是世界在你内在创造的心灵。它代表着你的文化的主要影响，以及你对世界的回应和反应。它包含着你的渴望、你的恐惧、你的遗憾、你的敌意、你的不原谅、你的倾向、你察觉的领域、你未察觉的领域等等。

Knowledge within you cannot be fooled, and it is immune from the forces of persuasion, wherever they might come from. This is the source of your strength, your integrity, your discernment and your personal power in the world. God has given you this perfect guiding Intelligence to guide you, to protect you and to lead you to the discovery of the greater purpose that has brought you into the world, and those key relationships that will be essential in helping you to discover and to express this greater purpose.

你内在的内识无法被愚弄，它不受任何说服力量的影响，无论它们来自何方。这是你的力量、你的完整性、你的辨别力以及你在世界上的个人力量的源泉。上帝赋予了你这个完美的指引智能，来指引你、保护你并引领你去发现将你带到这个世界的更伟大目的，以及那些在帮助你发现和表达这个更伟大目的方面至关重要的关键关系。

This is the great endowment from the Creator of all life. It will protect you in the mental environment because only Knowledge is free in the mental environment. It is not influenced by conventional thinking or religious views or government proclamations. It is not persuaded by powerful individuals who hold sway over you. It is not seduced by fear or desire or preference. It is beyond the dichotomy of love and fear, which is very confusing for most people since most people really do not understand what love is or what fear is, or where these come from.

这是来自所有生命创造者的伟大恩赐。它将在心理环境中保护你，因为在心理环境中唯有内识是自由的。它不受传统思维、宗教观点或政府宣言的影响。它不受那些左右你的强大个体的说服。它不受恐惧、渴望或偏好的诱惑。它超越爱与恐惧的二分法，这对大多数人来说非常令人困惑，因为大多数人真的不理解爱是什么，恐惧是什么，或者它们从何而来。

In order for you to think freely, creatively and independently, you must have Knowledge as your foundation. Otherwise, you are just mimicking other people, your culture or your circle of friends, your network of people that you identify with or associate with. Together you just reinforce each other’s preferences and prejudices. You reinforce each other’s weaknesses and blindness. You gather together for security and for reinforcement. You see this all around you, of course, as different groups of people associate with each other and reinforce each other’s perception of the world.

为了让你能够自由、创造性和独立地思考，你必须以内识为基础。否则，你只是在模仿其他人，你的文化或你的朋友圈，你认同或交往的人脉网络。你们在一起只会强化彼此的偏好和偏见。你们强化彼此的弱点和盲目。你们聚集在一起是为了安全感，也是为了强化。当然，你周围随处可见这种情况，不同的人群彼此联系，强化彼此对世界的感知。

But Knowledge within you really is the source of your clarity and your integrity as an individual. And yet it connects you with the Creator of all life because Knowledge is connected to God—not in a religious sense, not in the sense of religious teachings or religious institutions, for Knowledge will guide you whether you are participating in a religious faith tradition or not. It will guide you no matter what your religious faith, or even if you do not adhere to a religious faith.

可是你内在的内识确实是你作为个体的清晰和完整性的源泉。然而它却将你与所有生命的创造者联接，因为内识联接着上帝——并非以宗教意义，并非以宗教教义或宗教机构的意义，因为内识将指引你，无论你是否参与一个宗教信仰传统。无论你的宗教信仰是什么，甚至即使你不信奉宗教信仰，它都将指引你。

The Creator of all life is not bound by human understanding or human institutions or traditions of thought. This is your direct connection to God. This is how God communicates to you. This is how God assists you. And your ability to follow Knowledge, to experience Knowledge and to express what Knowledge will give you to express to the world represents your service to God.

所有生命的创造者不受人类理解、人类制度或思想传统的束缚。这是你与上帝的直接联接。这是上帝与你沟通的方式。这是上帝协助你的方式。你遵循内识、体验内识并表达内识将赋予你向世界表达之物的能力，代表着你对上帝的服务。

Yet people’s awareness of Knowledge is so weak and fleeting, or even nonexistent, that they cannot give this great confidence. To give the individual this much power and this much authority over their life seems very threatening to governments and religious institutions and to people who feel that the individual must be controlled and restrained to a great degree.

然而人们可能会因为他们的心灵被他们的社会、他们的家庭、他们的宗教以及他人的影响所占据而失去与此的联结。但这种更深层次的良知依然活在他们内在。即使他们已经看不见它，即使他们违背它，即使他们在世界上犯下可怕的罪行，这种更深层次的基础依然存在。他们只是失去了与它的联系。

And it casts great doubt over humanity’s future and well-being, and humanity’s sovereignty as a free race within this world.

人类如今正面临世界巨变的浪潮：环境破坏、资源枯竭、恶劣天气、经济和政治动荡以及战争风险。除此之外，人类还面临着一种巧妙而隐蔽的干预。所有这些对人类自由的挑战是如此巨大。它对人类的未来和福祉，以及人类作为这个世界上一个自由种族的主权，投下了巨大的疑虑。

这并不是要带你离开世界，或让你超越世界，而是要赋予你力量、能力和智慧，使你能够按照带你来到这里的更伟大目的，在世界上做出贡献。但首先你必须拥有自由去体验和发现这个目的；并重新调整你的生命朝向这个目的；并让你自己摆脱那些让你在这里被束缚、把你束缚在过去、阻止你认知你内在内识指引力量的义务、承诺和信仰。

上帝向世界发来了一条新讯息，让人类为变革的巨浪进行准备，让人类为宇宙生命的现实进行准备，在内识的层面上教授精神性，并向人类揭示它的未来和它的天命，以及它必须做出的伟大决策和对人类合作的伟大需求。

This rests upon the power of Knowledge within the individual, the liberating power of Knowledge. What prevents people from violence and degradation and cruelty is the power and presence of Knowledge within themselves.

这依赖于个体内在内识的力量，内识的解放力量。人类在任何时刻的福祉，都取决于人们对内识的觉知与其自身社会制约之间的平衡。阻止人们走向暴力、堕落和残忍的，正是内识的力量和存在。

In the future, there will be greater instability in your cultures and economies. What will keep people from preying upon one another, from attacking one another, from social violence and disorder? It will be the power and the presence of Knowledge. And what will give you the freedom to feel secure in an insecure world, and to find your direction while people all around you are in confusion and chaos? It is the power and the presence of Knowledge.

未来，你们的文化和经济将更加不稳定。什么将阻止人们彼此掠夺，彼此攻击，以及社会暴力和混乱？它是内识的力量和存在。什么将赋予你自由，让你在一个不安全的世界中感到安全，并在你周围的人们陷入困惑和混乱时找到你的方向？它是内识的力量和存在。

That is why God’s New Message has provided the preparation in The Way of Knowledge that people everywhere around the world can use, even today—by taking the Steps to Knowledge, by reconnecting with their deeper foundation, by being able to have their eyes and ears and senses liberated from the dominance of persuasion and manipulation in the mental environment.

正因为如此，上帝的新讯息在内识之路中提供了世界各地的人们即使在今天也能使用的准备——通过开展内识进阶，通过重新联接他们更深层次的基础，通过能够让他们的眼睛、耳朵和感官从心理环境中说服和操纵的支配中解放出来。

This is the key to your freedom and your fulfillment in the world and your ability to serve a world in need rather than merely condemning it. 这是通往你在世界上的自由和成就的关键，也是你服务于一个有需要的世界而非仅仅谴责它的能力的关键。这代表着你满足和成就的源泉，并将赋予你眼睛去看，赋予你感官去报告那些你注定要遇见的关系的存在，它们是你生命中更伟大目的的组成部分。

God has given you the perfect antidote to evil and the perfect answer to your needs. But you must experience this, and build a connection to this, and yield to this power, and allow it to free your mind and your perception. This is the greatest gift to you, and this will be your greatest gift to others who like you are seeking to find their freedom and their purpose and their true activities and destiny in the world.

上帝已经赋予你对抗邪恶的完美解药，以及满足你需求的完美答案。但你必须体验它，构建与它的连接，臣服于这种力量，并让它解放你的心灵和你的感知。这是赐予你的最伟大礼物，这也将是你给予其他像你一样正在寻求他们的自由、他们的目的、他们在世上真正的活动和天命的人最伟大的礼物。
