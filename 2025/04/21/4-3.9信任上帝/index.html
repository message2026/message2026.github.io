第九章：信任上帝

发表于2025-04-21|更新于2025-08-22|来自上帝的新讯息第四卷新的上帝体验
|总字数:9.6 k|阅读时长:40 分钟

Trusting God

As received by Marshall Vian Summers on May 7, 2013 in Boulder, Colorado
2013 年 5 月 7 日，在科罗拉多州博尔德(Boulder, Colorado)，向上帝的信使马歇尔・维安・萨默斯(Marshall Vian Summers)揭示

People have a great deal of difficulty trusting God. And based upon the notion of the old God, the judgmental God, the condemning God, their distrust is justified. But it is more complex than this.

人们很难信任上帝。基于对旧神、审判之神、谴责之神的观念，他们的不信任是有道理的。但实际情况远比这复杂得多。

For people do not know what to trust within themselves and one another, so their distrust permeates everything. And without the guidance of the deeper Knowledge that God has placed within them to guide them, they place their trust in the most unreliable things, the most temporary and uncertain things. They give their heart away to people, places and things, not knowing the great consequence of doing this and how difficult it will be for them later to reclaim their freedom and integrity once they have circumscribed themselves in such ways.

因为人们不知道在他们自己和他人内心深处该信任什么，所以他们的不信任渗透了一切。没有上帝放置在他们内在更深层次内识的指引，他们把信任放在最不可靠、最短暂、最不确定的事物上。他们把他们的心交给人、地方和事物，却不知道这样做的重大后果，也不知道一旦他们以这种方式限制自己，他们日后将多么难以重拾他们自身的自由和完整性。

It is difficult to trust that which one cannot see and touch. But it is also difficult to trust anything that is physical and changeable that can be destroyed or undermined by others. In this sense, what is invisible is actually more trustworthy, for it cannot be destroyed; it cannot be maligned; it cannot even be distorted except in the minds and thoughts of people, which is so often the case.

难以信任看不见摸不着的事物。但同样难以信任的是任何物质的、可变的、可能被他人破坏或破坏的东西。从这个意义上讲，看不见的东西实际上更值得信赖，因为它无法被摧毁；无法被诽谤；甚至无法被扭曲，除非在人们的心灵和思想中，而这种情况经常发生。

So trusting God becomes, then, the question of what one must trust within [oneself] and what one believes God to be and how God functions in the world, and the great problems and questions surrounding this, which have plagued humanity for as long as time. So We will address these things because they must be clarified. For if they cannot be clarified, the problems will exist, and trust will be based upon the weakest assumptions and foundation.

因此，信任上帝就变成了一个人必须在自身内信任什么，一个人相信上帝是什么，上帝如何在世界上运作，以及围绕着这些的重大问题和疑问，这些问题长久以来困扰着人类。因此我们将探讨这些问题，因为它们必须被澄清。因为如果它们无法被澄清，问题就会存在，信任就会建立在最薄弱的假设和基础上。

First of all, you must know that you must trust something in life. Here people tend to trust their firm beliefs and their admonitions, their personal opinions and their grievances against others, themselves and the world. Too afraid now to trust anything beyond themselves, they put all their faith in their notions, which are themselves rarely founded in reality and are rarely consistent and have no real permanence at all.

首先，你必须知道，你必须在生命中信任某些事物。在这里，人们倾向于信任他们坚定的信念、他们的劝诫、他们的个人观点以及他们对他人、对他们自己和世界的不满。如今，他们太害怕信任任何超越自身的事物，于是把所有的信任都寄托在他们的观念上，而这些观念本身很少建立在现实之上，很少前后一致，也根本没有真正的持久性。

Here people become fixated in their minds. Unable now to really experience life and to experience intimacy, closeness, resonance with another, they become bound to their ideas. Peering out through the little window in their concrete blockhouse, they try to see and relate to the world, a world that baffles them and frightens them and confuses them.

在这里，人们变得固着于他们的心灵。他们无法真正体验生命，无法体会亲密、亲近和与他人的共鸣，他们被束缚在他们的想法里。透过水泥碉堡里的小窗户，他们试图观察并理解这个世界，一个让他们困惑、恐惧、迷茫的世界。

The more fixed their ideas, the more they are imprisoned. The more bound up they are in their admonitions of others and their grievances against the world, the less able they are to experience the joys of life—the spaciousness of this moment, the miracle of life occurring all around them and the blessed presence, power and endowment that God has placed within them, which exists beyond the realm and the reach of the intellect.

他们的想法越是固执，就越是被禁锢。他们越是被束缚于对他人的劝诫和对世界的不满，就越是无法体验生命的喜悦——此刻的广阔，周围发生的生命奇迹，以及上帝赋予他们内在的祝福存在、力量和禀赋，这些超越了智力的领域和范围。

This is the condition of humanity, circumscribed not only by poverty and degradation, which is so prevalent around the world but also bound up in their beliefs, fears and admonitions. They cannot see; they cannot hear; they cannot know the truth.

这就是人类的现状，它不仅受限于世界各地普遍存在的贫困和堕落，也与人类的信仰、恐惧和警示息息相关。他们看不见，听不见，无法知晓真理。

God understands this, of course, because when you are separated from God and separated from Creation, living in the physical reality, you are also separated from the source of your inner strength, power and integrity that God has placed within you—an inescapable reality that lives within you every moment, waiting to be discovered, waiting to be followed. For only here can you find your way out of the maze, out of the jungle, out of the massive confusion that surrounds you and permeates your thinking and attitudes.

当然，上帝理解这一点，因为当你与上帝分离，与创造分离，生活在物质现实中时，你也与上帝放置在你内在的实力、力量和完整性的源泉分离——一个每时每刻都活在你内在的不可逃避的现实，等待着被发现，等待着被追随。因为唯有在这里，你才能找到走出迷宫、走出丛林、走出包围你并渗透你的思维和态度的巨大困惑的道路。

Heaven is all around you, but you cannot feel it; you cannot yet see it; you cannot yet hear it because your mind is caught up in itself—in your ideas, in other people’s ideas, in the influences around you and your own conditioning and culture and even religion itself.

天国就在你周围，但你无法感受到它；你还无法看到它；你还无法听到它，因为你的心灵陷入了它自身——陷入了你的想法，陷入了其他人的想法，陷入了你周围的影响以及你自己的条件和文化，甚至宗教本身。

People believe in God but cannot hear God or feel God or experience God, and so they place all of their emphasis on the firmness of their beliefs and the defense of their ideas. And though they may claim their whole life is for God and religion, they are very far away from their Source.

人们相信上帝，但却无法听到上帝，感受上帝，体验上帝，因此他们把所有的重点都放在坚定他们的信仰和捍卫他们的想法上。尽管他们可能声称他们的一生都是为了上帝和宗教，但他们离他们的本源却非常遥远。

To know God is to come to find that power and presence that God has placed within you, for only here will you feel and experience the resonance, the power and the purpose that are the true content and direction of your life. For who you really are is determined by Heaven and not by the world. What you are here to really do is determined by Heaven and those who sent you here, not by the world and all of its circumscribing events and influences, powers and pressures.

知道上帝，就是去发现上帝放置在你内在的那个力量和存在，因为唯有在此，你才能感受到并体验到那共鸣、那力量和目的，它们才是你生命的真正内容和方向。因为你真正是谁，是由天国而非世界决定的。你在此真正要做什么，是由天国以及那些派遣你来此的存在决定的，而非由世界以及它所有限制性的事件和影响、力量和压力决定的。

This is how God redeems you—not through belief, not through prostration, not through the firm adherence to fixed notions and ideas, for that does not get you across the bridge of light. With all these things, you are still standing on the foreign shore. And while God calls to you, you cannot hear because your mind is locked and fixed, bound as if you were shackled to a wall and could not leave your restraints.

上帝就是这样救赎你的——不是通过信仰，不是通过卑躬屈膝，不是通过对固有观念和想法的固执，因为那并不能让你跨越光明之桥。带着所有这些，你依然站在陌生的彼岸。当上帝呼唤你时，你却听不见，因为你的心灵被锁住、被束缚，仿佛被铐在墙上，无法摆脱束缚。

You must know what to trust within yourself and others, and it cannot be your ideas alone. For if they are not grounded in Knowledge itself, then they are a product of culture and influence and your own fearful preferences and persuasions.

你必须知道在你自己和他人内心深处该信任什么，而这不能仅仅是你的想法。因为如果它们并非根植于内识本身，那么它们就是文化、影响以及你自身恐惧的偏好和说服的产物。

Here the person who has no notion of religion may actually be closer to this Power and Presence than those who base their entire lives upon it. For God knows the way to God. And God has put the Power and the Presence within you to guide you, to protect you and to prepare you for a greater life of service in the world. This lives both within religion and beyond it, available to all.

在这里，那些对宗教毫无概念的人，或许实际上比那些将整个生命建立在宗教之上的人更接近这种力量和存在。因为上帝知道通往上帝的道路。上帝将这力量和存在置于你的内在，来指引你、保护你，并让你为服务世界的一个更伟大的生命进行准备。这既存在于宗教之内，也存在于宗教之外，面向所有人。

For God is not persuaded by your religious beliefs. If you cannot follow and find this greater endowment, you are still lost in the world, lost in Separation—separation from your deeper nature, separation from the power of Knowledge that God has given you, separate from others, separate from yourself, living in an island even in the midst of others.

因为上帝不会被你的宗教信仰所说服。如果你无法追随并找到这更伟大的禀赋，你依然迷失在世界中，迷失在分离中——与你更深层次的自然分离，与上帝赋予你的内识力量分离，与他人分离，与你自己分离，即使在他人之中也活在孤岛之中。

Only God knows how to liberate you from this seemingly hopeless situation. God has provided the Steps to Knowledge in a New Revelation for the world—pure, clear, unadulterated, unaltered by human adoption or manipulation, every word sacred and powerful from the Source, to give humanity another chance as it stands at the threshold of a changing world and a universe full of intelligent life.

唯有上帝知道如何将你从这看似无望的境况中解放出来。上帝为世界提供了一个新启示中的内识进阶——纯粹、明晰、无杂质，未被人类采纳或操控所改变，每一个文字都神圣而有力，来自源头，赋予人类另一个机会，因为人类正站在一个不断变化的世界和一个充满智能生命的宇宙的门槛上。

The essence of religion becomes lost as the ideology and the institutions are built around it. It becomes lost as people attempt to use ideas, belief and persuasion to force people into adherence and conformity.

当意识形态和制度围绕宗教建立起来时，宗教的本质就消失了。当人们试图用想法、信仰和说服来强迫他人皈依和顺从时，宗教的本质就消失了。

To be able to trust God, you must know what to trust within yourself and others. This then places the pathway right before you. It is not just having the right ideas though that is helpful and may be necessary at the outset. You must have the correct orientation, you see, and you must be able to think independently of the persuasions that exist around you that would want you to conform to the ideas and notions of culture and religion.

为了能够信任上帝，你必须知道在你自己和他人内心深处该信任什么。这样，你面前的道路就会变得清晰。虽然有正确的想法会有所帮助，而且在开始时可能是必要的，但这并不仅仅是有正确的想法。你必须拥有正确的方向，你看，你必须能够独立思考，不受周围那些想要你遵从文化和宗教思想和观念的说服力的影响。

You must look upon the world without condemnation, or you will be blind, for judgments and grievances are blinding. No matter how correct you may think you are in exercising these things, they will prevent you from seeing, knowing and understanding.

你必须不带谴责地看待这个世界，否则你就会变得盲目，因为评判和怨恨会让人盲目。无论你认为你在做这些事情时多么正确，它们都会阻碍你去看见、去知道、去理解。

Thus, you will have no security in the world. For security can only really be established by seeing, knowing and understanding in alignment with the deeper power that God has given you, which will teach you what to value and what not to value, what to listen to within yourself and listen to within others, how to manage your mind so it does not run like a crazy animal, learning how to think independently of others so that you may be free to think upon your own, thus using the power of your intellect effectively and appropriately as it was intended to be used.

因此，你在世界上将没有安全感。因为安全感只有通过与上帝赋予你的更深层次力量相一致地看见、知道和理解才能真正建立，这将教导你珍视什么，不珍视什么，倾听你内在的声音，倾听他人内在的声音，如何管理你的心灵，这样它就不会像一只疯狂的动物一样奔跑，学习如何独立于他人思考，这样你就能自由地独立思考，从而有效且恰当地运用你的智力力量，就像它原本的用途一样。

Next, you must look at what you think God is and how you think God functions in the world, or whether you even think God exists at all. This has a great deal to do with who you think you are, even at the level of thought.

接下来，你必须思考你认为上帝是什么，以及你认为上帝如何在世间运作，或者你是否认为上帝存在。这很大程度上关系到你认为你是谁，甚至在思想层面上也是如此。

If you think you are just a body, well, how can you account for God, who seems to have no body. God is a nobody. And you do not want to be a nobody, do you? If you think you are your thoughts and your mind, well, you will not be able to comprehend the Mind and the Will and the Presence of God, for the mind is a vehicle of communication in the world. It is not your true nature. It is not who you really are.

如果你认为你只是一具躯体，好吧，你又如何解释上帝呢？上帝似乎没有躯体。上帝无足轻重。而你也不想成为无足轻重的人，对吧？如果你认为你就是你的思想和你的心灵，那么，你将无法理解上帝的心灵、意志和存在，因为心灵是沟通世界的工具。它并非你的真实自然。它并非真正的你。

When you leave this world, well, you will not have any beliefs. You will just be present, as you were before you came into the world. So belief cannot bring you to God. Belief cannot enable you to cross the bridge of light.

当你离开这个世界时，嗯，你将没有任何信仰。你只是在场，就像你来到这个世界之前一样。所以信仰无法带你走向上帝。信仰无法让你跨越光明之桥。

Yet you cannot really be without beliefs of some kind, so you use them as a temporary structure to help orient your thinking and your behavior and your activities in the world, and that is appropriate. But the greater questions must be left open, and this must take you into the realm of real experience and not merely thinking and speculation.

然而你无法真正没有某种信仰，因此你使用它们作为一个临时结构来帮助引导你的思考、你的行为和你在世界上的活动，这是恰当的。但更伟大的问题必须保持开放，这必须带你进入真实体验的领域，而非仅仅是思考和猜测。

To have a sense of God, you must look at what you think you are. You must have a correct understanding of your relationship with your mind and your body. Here thinking and understanding are very important because they give you the window of opportunity to see what you could not see before, and to correctly understand how you are to utilize the mind and the body in life, and how they can serve you and not merely infringe upon you.

要感知上帝，你必须审视你认为的你是什么。你必须正确理解你与你的心灵和身体的关系。在这里，思维和理解非常重要，因为它们为你提供了机会之窗，让你看到过去无法看到的事情，并让你正确地理解如何在生命中运用心灵和身体，以及它们如何服务于你，而非仅仅侵犯你。

For the mind without Knowledge is like a crown of thorns. While it may give you recognition in the world, it is painful and uncomfortable. And you will seek escape from it in pleasures and addictions and fixations of all kinds.

因为缺乏内识的心灵如同荆棘冠冕。它或许能让你获得世间的认可，却又让你痛苦难受。你会在各种享乐、沉迷和执着中寻求解脱。

If God is Spirit and not merely form, or a concept, or a hierarchy of concepts, or a belief system, then you must go beyond these things in your approach. And God has given you the Steps to Knowledge to take you beyond the limits of your thinking and the restraints you have placed upon yourself and have allowed others to place upon you to give you this opportunity to see, to know and to understand.

如果上帝是精神，而非仅仅是形式，或一种概念，或一个概念层级，或一个信仰体系，那么你必须在你的路径中超越这些东西。上帝赋予了你内识进阶，带你超越你思维的局限，以及你强加于你自己以及允许他人强加于你的限制，从而赋予你这个去看、去知道和去理解的机会。

So you must look at what you think you are. Here understanding is important because if you cannot see the correct relationship of things, then you will confuse them terribly, and your approach will be skewed and uncertain. This will cast you back into confusion, a deep confusion out of which all your errors and misconceptions arise.

因此，你必须审视你认为的你是谁。在这里，理解至关重要，因为如果你无法看清事物之间的正确关系，你就会把它们弄得一团糟，你的研究方法也会变得偏颇和不确定。这会让你再次陷入困惑，一种深深的困惑，你所有的错误和误解都源于此。

This is for you to consider. But remember if you think you are only a body, then you will feel as frail and vulnerable in the world as your body really is. If you think you are only a mind, you will use the mind to try to defend itself, to defend its ideas, even to the point of going to war with others and creating hatred, murder and mayhem to protect your ideas. You will live in fear and uncertainty and seek to counteract this with pride and arrogance in your thinking, your logic, which is merely an attempt to adapt yourself to an uncertain world.

这是你需要深思熟虑的。但记住，如果你认为你只是一具身体，那么你将感受到你在这个世界上如同你的身体一样脆弱无助。如果你认为你只是一个心灵，你将用心灵来试图捍卫它自身，捍卫它的想法，甚至不惜与他人开战，制造仇恨、谋杀和混乱，来保护你的想法。你将生活在恐惧和不确定性之中，并试图用你的思维、你逻辑中的骄傲和傲慢来抵消它，而这仅仅是试图让你自己适应一个不确定的世界。

You must see yourself existing beyond these things and learn how to utilize the mind and the body as great vehicles of communication. For without them, you could not communicate with others in the world. You could not give your gifts to the world. Without these, you may have the wisdom of the universe, but no one could hear you or see you or understand your demonstration.

你必须看到你自己超越这些事物而存在，并学习如何运用心灵和身体作为沟通的伟大载体。因为没有它们，你就无法与世人沟通。你就无法将你的礼物给予世界。没有这些，你或许拥有宇宙的智慧，但没有人能够听到你、看到你或理解你的展现。

The third thing that must be considered is the relationship with God and your experience in the world and the world itself. This is where a great many problems arise. People think God is the author of everything that happens in the world. Well, that cannot be a loving God. That cannot be a wise God. That cannot be a compassionate God.

第三件必须考虑的事情是与上帝的关系，以及你在世上的经历，以及这个世界本身。很多问题都源于此。人们认为上帝是世间万物的创造者。那么，这不可能是一位慈爱的上帝。这不可能是一位明智的上帝。这也不是一位富有同情心的上帝。

With children dying of cancer and nations of people starving to death and civil war and strife arising with increasing frequency, do you make God the author of these things? You certainly could not trust such a God. You certainly could not love such a God. You certainly could not follow such a God.

儿童死于癌症，国家饿死人，内战纷争日益频繁，难道你会认为上帝是这一切的始作俑者吗？你当然不会信任这样的上帝。你当然不会爱这样的上帝。你当然不会追随这样的上帝。

The suffering, the conflict, the degradation you see in the world amongst the rich and the poor are the product of human confusion and misunderstanding. [They are] the product of people living without the power and presence of Knowledge to guide them, to bless them and to correctly place them in relation with others. [They are] the product of living in Separation—divorced from yourself, divorced from your Source, divorced from the center of your spiritual power, integrity, certainty and direction. Of course, the world will look like this. It is a miracle it does not look worse.

你在富人和穷人之间看到的苦难、冲突和堕落，是人类困惑和误解的产物。[它们是]人们生活在缺乏内识的力量和存在来指引他们、祝福他们并正确地将他们置于与他人的关系中的产物。[它们是]生活在分离中的产物——与你自己分离，与你的本源分离，与你的精神力量、完整性、确定性和方向的中心分离。当然，世界将会是这个样子。它没有变得更糟真是个奇迹。

People are good and bad, noble and pathetic, representing the whole gamut of qualities—desirable and undesirable. Who can make sense of this? Did God create such a conflicted creature? Did God place you in such a difficult and uncertain environment? Did God cast you down from Heaven to live in a world of ever-changing circumstances, of great peril and uncertainty?

人有善有恶，有高尚有可悲，代表着各种各样的品质——可取的和不可取的。谁能理解这一点？上帝创造了如此矛盾的生物吗？上帝把你置于如此艰难和不确定的环境中吗？上帝把你从天堂驱逐，让你生活在一个充满变幻莫测、危机四伏和不确定性的世界吗？

Here a correct understanding gives you the opportunity to see the relationship of things differently. Here understanding is important though it is but one component of what will enable you to rise above the misery of your past and the misery you see all around you, in the rich and in the poor.

在这里，正确的理解让你有机会以不同的方式看待事物之间的关系。在这里，理解很重要，尽管它只是帮助你超越过去的苦难以及你周围——无论贫富——所见苦难的一个组成部分。

God does not run the weather of the world. God is not moving the blood through your veins. God is not pouring the water over the cliff. God is not sprouting the seed in the ground. That was all set in motion at the beginning of time, the beginning of the Separation—the very Separation that has brought you to the world and keeps you here until you can learn to follow the power and presence that God has placed within you, which is available to all—to all peoples, all religions, the human family and the countless races in the universe that are living in the separated existence with you. What created the Separation is beyond your comprehension, as is the Separation itself, but you live with its effects every day.

上帝并非掌控世界的气候。上帝并非在你的血管中流动血液。上帝并非将水倾泻到悬崖之下。上帝并非在地里催生种子。这一切都在时间之初，在分离之初就已启动——正是这种分离将你带到了世界，并让你留在这里，直到你能够学习遵循上帝放置在你内在的力量和存在，它面向所有人——面向所有民族、所有宗教、人类大家庭以及宇宙中与你一起生活在分离存在中的无数种族。造成分离的原因超出你的理解，分离本身也是如此，但你每天都生活在它的影响之中。

So do not blame God for the events of the world. Do not blame God for illness or misfortune. Do not think that everything is happening according to a Divine Plan, as if every event has meaning, purpose and value for you.

因此，不要把世间发生的事归咎于上帝。不要把疾病或不幸归咎于上帝。不要以为一切都是按照神圣的计划发生的，仿佛每件事对你来说都有意义、目的和价值。

The world is chaotic and uncertain. Everything can change in a moment. There are no guarantees. That is why God has given you the great endowment of Knowledge, for it is the only thing that is truly certain, pure and efficacious in the world.

这个世界混乱而不确定。一切都可能瞬间改变。没有任何保证。正因如此，上帝赋予你内识的伟大禀赋，因为它是世界上唯一真正确定、纯粹和有效的东西。

Learn to recognize this in your experience. Learn to value this above your desires and your fears. Learn to recognize how you have lived life without Knowledge and the terrible cost this has been for you and the great prices you have paid through your errors and misunderstanding. And you will come to value this, finally, for it is the one true hope and promise of your life.

学习在你的体验中认知这一点。学习重视这一点，超越你的渴望和恐惧。学习认知你如何在没有内识的情况下生活，以及这给你带来的可怕代价，以及你因你的错误和误解而付出的巨大代价。最终，你将开始重视这一点，因为它是你生命中唯一真正的希望和承诺。

God saves the separated through Knowledge. The Lord of the entire universe and all dimensions of the universe is not running the events of your life because God is intelligent. God is giving you the freedom to live in Separation because that was your initial decision, you see. But to live in this environment is very perilous, and therefore God has given you the great endowment. That in the end is the greatest gift of all, but you will only see this and know this if you can recognize it, value it and learn to follow it, taking the Steps to Knowledge.

上帝通过内识拯救分离者。整个宇宙以及宇宙所有维度的上帝并未掌控你生命的事件，因为上帝是智慧的。上帝赋予你自由去生活在分离里，因为那是你最初的决定，你看。但是生活在这种环境中非常危险，因此上帝赋予了你伟大的禀赋。这最终是所有礼物中最伟大的，但只有当你能够认出它、珍视它并学习遵循它，开展内识进阶时，你才能看到它并知道它。

This is the beginning of the return, not to leave the world but to bring a greater life into the world—the life you were meant to live here, the life you are not yet living, the life you must live to find true happiness and fulfillment here.

这是回归的开始，并非离开世界，而是将一个更伟大生命带到世界上——你注定在此过的生命，你尚未过的生命，你必须过的生命，才能在此找到真正的幸福和成就。

God set in motion the forces, the geological and biological forces that have taken great amounts of time to come into being before human beings could even be in this world. But time is nothing to God though it is everything to you.

上帝启动了各种力量，包括地质和生物的力量，它们在人类诞生之前，经历了漫长的时间才得以形成。然而，时间对上帝来说微不足道，而对你来说却至关重要。

So do not blame God for the conditions of life, even the harshness of nature, which seems to have no real regard for you and your welfare. Do not blame God for the hurricanes and the earthquakes, the pestilence and the famines, for that is just nature functioning according to its own rules and guidelines.

因此，不要因为生命的境况，甚至大自然的严酷，而责怪上帝，它似乎对你和你的福祉毫不关心。不要因为飓风、地震、瘟疫和饥荒而责怪上帝，因为那只是大自然按照其自身的规律和准则运转而已。

God is standing apart watching to see who can respond to the greater power that has been given them. And God’s Angelic Assembly watches over the world to assist individuals who are at great turning points in their lives so that they may have the chance to find the source of their redemption, which lives within them at every moment. And your Spiritual Family sends messages to you to help you though you may not hear much of what they have to offer.

上帝独处一隅，注视着谁能回应那被赋予的更伟大力量。上帝的天使圣团注视着世界，帮助那些正处于人生重大转折点的人们，使他们有机会找到救赎的源泉，这源泉时刻存在于他们内心。你的精神家庭会向你发送讯息来帮助你，尽管你可能听不到他们能提供多少帮助。

That will come in time as your mind settles down, as it relieves itself of its crown of thorns, as it learns to watch nature and events of the world with compassion, and to recognize that everything that happens, even the most grievous events, are a demonstration of the great need for Knowledge—in your life and in the world as a whole.

随着你的心灵安定下来，随着它卸下荆棘冠冕，随着它学会带着同情去观察自然和世界事件，并认识到所发生的一切，即使是最悲痛的事件，都是对内识的伟大需求的示范——在你的生命中以及在整个世界中——这将及时到来。

God is pure and beyond these things, but God has placed within you something that is pure and beyond these things that cannot be corrupted, that cannot be defiled, that cannot even be misused. It can only be misunderstood, and in this people will commit many errors. But the power and the purity exist within them, beyond their attempt to manipulate it for their own benefit.

上帝是纯粹的，超越这些，但上帝在你内在放置了某种纯粹的、超越这些的内识，它无法被腐化，无法被玷污，甚至无法被滥用。它只能被误解，并且人们会因此犯下许多错误。但那力量和纯粹存在于他们内在，超越了他们为了自身利益而操纵它的企图。

For God knows in the end you will turn to Knowledge. In this life or beyond, you will turn to Knowledge, for you can never leave it. It is the guarantee of your redemption, however far in the future that may be. It is the only thing in life that is ultimately trustworthy—Knowledge within you, Knowledge within others, relationships built upon Knowledge and higher purpose. These are the most certain things in life, for everything else can change and will change in ways you cannot yet predict.

因为上帝知道，最终你将转向内识。在今生或来世，你将转向内识，因为你永远无法离开它。它是你救赎的保证，无论未来多么遥远。它是生命中唯一最终值得信赖的东西——你内在的内识，他人内在的内识，以及建立在内识和更高宗旨之上的关系。这些是生命中最确定的事情，因为其他一切都可能改变，并且将以你尚无法预测的方式改变。

To free yourself at last from constant anxiety and uncertainty, to liberate yourself from the constraints of your fixed ideas, this will be your journey to real freedom—emotional freedom, intellectual freedom, circumstantial freedom—which you have sought hopelessly in so many other ways. God’s New Revelation has made this very clear and has repeated it over and over again so that you will have the opportunity to hear it and finally see it and to recognize its essential importance in your life.

最终将你自己从持续不断的焦虑和不确定性中解放出来，将你自己从固有想法的束缚中解放出来，这将是你通往真正自由的旅程——情感自由、智力自由、环境自由——你曾以许多其他方式无望地追寻这些自由。上帝的新启示已将这一点阐明得清清楚楚，并一遍又一遍地重复，以便你有机会聆听它，最终亲眼目睹它，并认识到它在你生命中至关重要的重要性。

Here you will know that God loves you and cares for you, for the guarantee of your redemption has been placed within you no matter how grievous your life has been, no matter how deep your regrets. And if you have committed violence in the world, well, it will just take a little longer for you to come to the power and presence that lives within you, for your shame will be deeper. Your regrets will be deeper. Your pain will be deeper. But only Knowledge within you can dispel these things in time and give you the strength to compensate for them by living a true and efficacious life.

在这里，你将知道上帝爱你、关心你，因为你救赎的保证已被置于你的内在，无论你的生命多么悲痛，无论你的悔恨多么深刻。如果你在世界上犯下了暴力，那么，你只需要更长时间才能触及你内在的力量和存在，因为你的羞耻将更深。你的悔恨将更深。你的痛苦将更深。但唯有你内在的内识能够及时驱散这些事情，并赋予你力量，通过过一个真实而有效的生命来弥补它们。

People may argue the points of religion. People may debate endlessly, and have debated endlessly, the meaning of God and the reality of God. But the Revelation of this exists in the realm of pure experience, for ideas can only point you in a direction. They cannot get you up the mountain. For that you need inner strength and determination, not merely ideas or firm beliefs.

人们或许会争论宗教的要点。人们或许会无休止地辩论，并且已经无休止地辩论，关于上帝的含义和上帝的现实。但这种启示存在于纯粹体验的领域，因为想法只能为你指明方向。它们无法带你登上高峰。为此，你需要内在的力量和决心，而不仅仅是想法或坚定的信念。

Let the scholars stand at the bottom of the mountain and argue and debate the meaning of their ideas and their traditions, while those who are truly courageous will begin to venture up the great slopes. For it is only as you reach a higher vantage point on this mountain can you really see the lay of the land and understand the nature of your true journey in life, and where it was meant to take you, and who sent you on this journey, and who watches over you and has watched over you, and what you are really here to do, to see and to know.

让学者们站在山脚下，争论辩论他们的想法和传统的意义，而那些真正勇敢的人，将开始冒险攀登雄伟的山坡。因为只有当你登上这座山峰更高的制高点时，你才能真正看清地势，理解你生命真正旅程的本质，理解它注定要带你去往何方，是谁派你踏上这段旅程，是谁守护着你，并且一直在守护着你，以及你来此的真正目的是什么，去看见，去知道。

Therefore, you must learn what to trust within yourself. You must consider who you think you are and what you think you are and understand your true relationship with your mind and your body in a very compassionate way. You must understand the nature of God and the difference between God and what you see in the physical world, even in the natural world.

因此，你必须学习在你自己的内心深处信任什么。你必须思考你认为你是谁，你认为你是什么，并以一种非常同情的方式理解你与你的心灵和你的身体的真正关系。你必须理解上帝的自然，以及上帝与你在物质世界，甚至自然世界所见之物之间的区别。

Do not call nature God because this will be inadequate. Though it is grand and though it can be exalting to consider, God is even greater than this. It takes you beyond, beyond—beyond your thoughts, beyond your new thoughts, beyond your old thoughts, beyond, into a realm of recognition that is clear and unadulterated and uncorrupted by your experience of being in the world.

不要称自然为上帝，因为这不够充分。尽管它宏大，尽管思考它可能会令人崇高，但上帝甚至比这更伟大。它带你超越，超越——超越你的思想，超越你的新思想，超越你的旧思想，超越，进入一个清澈、纯粹、未被你存在于世的体验所腐蚀的认知境界。

God therefore takes you to where trust is obvious and deep and profound and powerful enough to live in a world of uncertainty, in a world where people are still lost all around you—lost in their fear, lost in their hope, lost in their degradation and their circumstances.

因此，上帝带你去往一个信任显而易见、深刻、深远且足够强大的地方，从而能够生活在一个不确定的世界里，在一个人们依然在你周围迷失的世界里——迷失在他们的恐惧里，迷失在他们的希望里，迷失在他们的堕落和他们的境遇里。

Do not condemn them, for they show you the state you are now attempting to escape. And only God has given you the means to do this, given now in a clear form, given now so that you may find what you truly came here to do.

不要谴责他们，因为他们向你展示了你现在试图逃避的状态。只有上帝赋予了你实现这个意义的途径，现在就以清晰的形式给予你，现在就给你，以便你能够找到你来这里真正要做的事情。
